Why Mission in Cambodia

  • The Door is Open

    Cambodia is still very open to have Christian missionaries come. There is no better time to go than now

  • Church Growing

    Churches in Cambodia are still in their early stage of formation. It would be a real blessing and joy for us to visit, encourage, and pray for the local Christians

  • The Future

    50% of the population is under 22 years old. We hope to reach out to these young people who are the future of the country

Our Mission

"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit" - Matthew 28:19

In short our mission is to learn about what God is doing in Cambodia and be part of his work there, especially among the young population of the country through partnerships with the local churches and missionaries.

Our team first went to Cambodia during the winter break of 2014 and has gone back every winter break since then. Many of the team members have been involved in children, youth and young adult ministries. And God has opened doors for us in Cambodia to work with local churches and ministries that reach out to these same groups of people – the young who are the future of the country.

The reason we go during the winter break is that we would like to have university students join the trip.

For each team member, this short mission trip is a part of our discipleship journey as a disciple of Jesus. We follow the examples of Jesus, and the early Christians such as Paul, Peter, Timothy, who were willing to share the gospel with those who are different from them in an unfamiliar environment.

This mission trip is such a transforming journey. On the one hand, we – the goers - are being transformed. We go there with an open heart and attitude to learn through what we see, the individuals we listen to, and all that we pray for. On the other hand, by God’s grace we are transforming lives there through our visiting, sharing, listening, and praying.
